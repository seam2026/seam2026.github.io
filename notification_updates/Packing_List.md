# Packing List for SEAM 2026

**Event Location:** Grischalodge, Parpan, Switzerland (1,450m Alpine elevation)
**Dates:** 1st - 4th May 2026
**Weather:** Expect temperatures between subzero to under 13°C, with cloudy skies and possible rain or snow

## Essential Items

**Towels & Toiletries (MUST BRING):**
* **2-3 Towels** (bath towels - NOT provided by venue)
* Washcloth/hand towel
* **Toiletries:** toothbrush, toothpaste, shampoo, conditioner, soap, shower gel
* **Personal Care:** deodorant, skincare products, sanitary items/pads (if needed), any personal feminine hygiene products
* Any personal medications or supplements
* Glasses/contacts if needed

*Note: Blankets, pillows, and bedding are provided by the venue. You do NOT need to bring these.*

**White Dress (For Group Gatherings & Bhajan Sessions):**
* **3-4 white outfits** minimum (the event includes 6 major Bhajan hall gatherings where white dress is preferred):
  - Friday evening session (Welcome & Bhajans)
  - Saturday morning session (Bhajans)
  - Saturday evening session (Musical Offering & Bhajans)
  - Sunday morning session (Bhajans)
  - Sunday evening session (Drama & Bhajans)
  - Monday morning closing session (Aarati & Closing)

**Winter/Cold Weather Gear:**
* **Warm jacket or fleece coat** (essential - temperatures can reach subzero, max of 14 degrees Celsius)
* **Sweaters/cardigans** (2-3 for layering)
* **Thermal leggings/warm base layers**
* **Warm pants or jeans** (2-3 pairs)
* **Warm socks** (at least 4-5 pairs)
* **Winter hat/beanie**
* **Scarf or neck warmer**
* **Warm gloves/mittens** (important for outdoor activities)

**Outdoor Activity Gear:**
* **Waterproof jacket or rain jacket** (for Day 2 outdoor breakout sessions and Day 3 trip to Lake Heidsee & Squirrel Park)
* (We might expect surprise rains or snow from the weather forecasts)
* **Comfortable walking/hiking shoes** (waterproof recommended)
* **Umbrella or rain protection**
* **Backpack** (for outdoor excursions)

**Regular Clothing:**
* **Casual pants/trousers** (2-3 pairs, non-white)
* **Comfortable shirts/tops** (3-4)
* **Light sweaters or long sleeves** (for layering)
* **Undergarments** (4-5 sets)
* **Pajamas/sleepwear** (2 sets)
* **Comfortable shoes/slippers** (for indoor wear)

**Miscellaneous:**
* Personal documents/ID/passport
* Phone and charger
* **Power Adapters (Switzerland uses Type C/E plugs - different from rest of Europe)** - highly recommended
* Small bag/purse for daily items
* Notebook and pen
* Camera (if desired)
* Sunscreen and lip balm with SPF (Alpine sun can be strong despite cold)
* Any books or personal items

**Mobile Connectivity & Roaming:**
* **Important:** Roaming charges can be extremely costly. We strongly recommend getting an eSIM before arrival.
* **Recommended eSIM providers with Switzerland coverage:**
  - **[Airalo (Tschüss Plan)](https://www.airalo.com/switzerland-esim):** Widely considered the gold standard for travel eSIMs. This Switzerland-specific plan is incredibly reliable, running on the Sunrise/Salt networks. It usually costs around $5 to $10 USD for 1GB to 3GB of data, which is plenty for 4 days of Google Maps and WhatsApp.
  - **[Holafly](https://esim.holafly.com/esim-switzerland/):** The best option for heavy data users and video streaming. Holafly offers unlimited data plans for Switzerland for around $15 to $20 USD for 5 days. Note: Holafly often restricts hotspot tethering.
  - **[Nomad](https://www.getnomad.app/switzerland-esim) or [Ubigi](https://cellulardata.ubigi.com/rates-and-coverage/switzerland-data-plans/):** Excellent, reliable alternatives to Airalo with competitive pricing and highly user-friendly apps. Ubigi is especially great if you plan to cross the border into France or Germany, as they have fantastic regional European plans.
  - **Local Prepaid ([Sunrise](https://www.sunrise.ch/en/residential/mobile/prepaid) / [Swisscom](https://www.swisscom.ch/en/residential/mobile/prepaid.html)):** If you prefer going directly through a local provider, both Sunrise and Swisscom offer prepaid tourist eSIMs. Swisscom has the absolute best network coverage in the high Alps, though it will be slightly more expensive than Airalo.

## Notes
* **Be prepared for surprise rain or snow** - keep waterproof gear easily accessible
* **Layering is key** for the variable Alpine weather
* The venue does NOT provide towels - you MUST bring your own. Blankets, pillows, and bedding ARE provided.
* Jenatsch Lodge (separate accommodation across the road) will have similar packing needs
* Pack items you can carry comfortably; it's a 4-day event, not a long trek
* Consider the elevation (1,450m) - weather can be unpredictable and can change quickly

## Transportation Tips
* If arriving by train: consider a lightweight rolling suitcase
* If driving: you can pack more comfortably
* Keep valuables and important documents in carry-on/personal bag
